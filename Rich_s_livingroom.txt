Miss Pi - (Technical asstant that has no write access to the server)
Raspberry Pi 5 16GB RAM in Freenove case secured to a 27" Dell monitor on an articulating arm, to keep her close.
She is attached under the USB Dell soundbar (bottom mounted to monitor), has a built in forward facing camaras and Solocast USB Microphone for voice input.
128GB NVMe 
Miss Pi is running nemotron-3-super:cloud on ollama pro subscription.
Using VS-Code SSH into Miss Pi using Continue to write all code and scripts for Miss Pi. 
The idea of Miss Pi came from wanting to have a second set of eyes look at everything I can see, while not having any access to keys or code.
For security reasons, because we run everything private, this is a far better approach than chatting directly on the server (freeing resource as well). 
Since, she's a second set of eyes, I thought I'd make it seem like she's literally sitting beside me.
She moves the mouse on screen, types and acts just like she was sitting next to me.
She has her finger on the pulse of the Holodeck through the data pipeline and makes sounds when events occurs or she wants to get my attention.
Her operational logic is presence-aware and proactive. When the camera detects a hand reaching into the interaction zone near the mute button, she treats this as a verified handshake protocol, pre-loading her context and preparing the Holodeck dashboard for immediate engagement. 
She maintains an active bridge status as long as the operator is detected within the desk zone, ensuring her local language models are ready but silent. 
When she detects that the operator has vacated the bridge zone, she initiates a three-minute departure protocol, concluding with an audible chirp. If no return is detected after one additional minute, she transitions to monitor dark mode. 
She is a purely local-only sentinel with zero custody or access to keys or code.
She has learning behavior and saves locally to (behavior_lib.json).
